Conditional Statement Practice Questions

1) Check whether the given number is even or odd.
2) Check whether the given number is positive or negative.
3) Check whether the given number is divisible by 5 or not.
4) Check whether the given number is a multiple of 3 and 5.
5) Find the largest number among the given three numbers.

6) Given marks of a student of 5 subjects check whether the student is pass or fail. (Passing marks: 40 in each subject)

7) Given the age of a person, check whether the person is eligible to vote or not. (Eligible age: 18)

8) Given the age of a person, check whether the person is a child, teenager, adult, or old.
For children: 0-12 years
For teenagers: 13-19 years
For adults: 20-59 years
For old: 60 and above

9) Given the marks of a student, check whether the student is excellent, good, average, or poor.
For excellent: marks >= 80
For good: 60 <= marks < 80
For average: 40 <= marks < 60
For poor: marks < 40

10) Given the three sides of a triangle, check whether the triangle is equilateral, isosceles, or scalene.
For equilateral: all sides are equal
For isosceles: any two sides are equal
For scalene: no sides are equal
